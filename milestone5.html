Informed consent form used



Questions for Interview

1. What is your experience related to epilepsy?
2. How do you feel about the rating process? (Ask after sign in/sign up and rating)
3. How do you feel about the video content? (After asking them to go to breakout room and watch content for ~5-10 minutes)
4. What other areas have you known epilepsy patients to struggle in, which our application does not cover?
5. Was there anything that stuck out to you about the platform?
6. What features would you build into a web application to augment your own work?
7. Do you know anyone else connected to epilepsy that you’d be willing to put us in touch with?
8. Any general UI issues?


Session observations
P1

1. URI professor whose research is on biomedical devices for treating/reducing seizures.
2. Main takeaway: epilepsy’s emotional consequences are directly related to the brain region where seizures take hold.

P2

1. Elementary school nurse currently dealing with a number of students who have epilepsy.
2. Best friend from high school was epileptic.
3. Main takeaway: young children don’t know yet that they “should” be embarrassed about their conditions.

P3

1. Head nurse of a MA school district.
2. Works with a number of students w/ epilepsy, especially epilepsy + special needs.
3. Main takeaway: has noticed with students that hormonal changes and sleep patterns have changed seizure frequency.


Usability lessons

Number Participant Issue Criticality
1 P1 Could not access the web application (even though we all could from different browsers). Why not? Highest
2 P1 The chart for emotional progress over time might not appeal to everyone. Medium
3 P3 The interface naturally excludes patients with behavioral/learning disorders. Medium
4 P1, P2 Lacking content from licensed physicians, as well as links to actually established, useful resources. Medium
5 P1 Different people have different learning styles. Videos won’t always be best method (add music, reading, …). Medium
6 P2 The log in and sign up pages are not distinct and clear from each other. The user managed to navigate through the pages in a very odd way, and did not sign up initially when intending to. Medium
7 P2 While our application is built to provide support for people with epilepsy, a large part of supporting people is community. There is no integration of community Medium
8 P2 Took more than a few seconds to realize how to use the sidebar navigation. Low
9 P3 No inclusion of information geared towards different age groups using the application (as epilepsy patients age, symptoms may change) low
10 P2 (There is an opportunity to help educate younger audiences, but we’re lacking support for them -- content is too advanced.) low

High-level reflections

1. Expertise in the source of the content.
2. Test the application multiple times to make sure it works every time.
3. The idea should be more comprehensive（users’ personal information, learning styles).
4. Give slightly less background information about the application so that when users interact with the website, they are operating out of instinct offering a more authentic user test
